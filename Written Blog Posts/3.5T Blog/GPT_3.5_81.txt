**Body:**

In the landscape of network dynamics, the pursuit of consensus plays a pivotal role across various domains such as robotics, distributed computing, and social networks. The paper by Cheng, Hou, and Tan delves into the complexities of high-order integral multi-agent systems, exploring how these agents can harmonize their states to reach a unified agreement amidst changing connections dictated by switching-directed topologies.

The study introduces two distributed protocols meticulously crafted to address the consensus problem within high-order integral multi-agent systems. These protocols are engineered to guide agents towards a shared stationary value, facilitating the convergence needed to achieve consensus. What sets this research apart is its emphasis on networks operating under dynamic switching-directed topologies, where the fluid nature of connections poses a unique challenge to consensus formation.

By presenting a robust consensus protocol complemented by a detailed performance analysis, the authors unravel the dynamics governing consensus formation within these intricate systems. Through insightful simulation examples, they showcase the practical effectiveness of their approach, demonstrating how agents can seamlessly reach a unified agreement despite the ever-changing network connections.

**Conclusion:**

The significance of this study transcends theoretical implications, offering a deeper understanding of achieving consensus in dynamic multi-agent systems.
